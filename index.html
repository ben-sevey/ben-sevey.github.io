About Me

Welcome! If you're looking for a creative, witty, unique, and memorable introduction then I'm sorry to dissappoint. I began to write such a piece, but alas I spend far too many words on saying far too little. Instead you can read a boring, informative paragraph about my general qualifications and idealogy as a programmer.

I have been writing programs since adding a Computer Science degree towards the end of my collegiate studies, but have only recently become serious about pursuing a career as a developer. I have spent the last two years post-graduation employed as an instructor in various roles, and I am now looking to transition from a hobbyist programmer to a full-time software developer. My recent focus has been on Front End Web Development, though I am open to any role that emphasizes problem-solving. What I enjoy most is the unique mindset inherent in programming, and using the tools at my disposal to create efficient, robust solutions. That being said, talk is cheap. Projects

Please take a moment to explore the few projects that I have worked on in the past 12 months. I personally wrote all code, with the exception being fraction.js within the linear system solver. I offer these projects as samples not just of my ability, but also of my dedication to producing tidy, efficient code. I invite you to examine the source code and hold my work accountable for what it does, as well as how it is written.

Atari Asteroids Programmed in Scratch (from scratch)

I worked at an afterschool program during the '15-16 school year aimed at developing 21st century skills. About a month after joining I discovered that a programming enrichment activity was offerred to our students, and I decided to share my expertise. I created an Asteroids knock-off hoping to spark interest and discuss basic game development practices with students.

Transforming Words to Pictures

After learning the basics of HTML/CSS I wanted to practice simple layouts and begin experimenting with Javascript. I turned to a project that had always interested me but one I had never invested time in: turning a set of text into a picture. The final product generates procedural patterns moreso than pictures, but who knows, maybe there's a market for turning poetry into tacky throw rugs.

Solving Systems of Linear Equations

While I continued my foray into front-end development, the middle school where I worked was learning how to solve basic algebra. Confronted with the challenge of delivering a failsafe procedure to solve single-variable equations I decided to build a web app to do just that. Things got a little out of hand and I ended with an NxM linear systems solver.
